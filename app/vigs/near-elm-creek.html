By Steven Speicher
April 5, 2015

Near Elm Creek

About 15 miles west of Kearney is the Elm Creek Complex, southeast of Elm Creek. The complex encompasses the Kearney Canal Diversion, a focused chokepoint on the river, just below the Elm Creek bridge.

Due to the nature of the diversion, the river is consolidated into a single flow, necessitating management for wider channels, approximating the width of the river prior to 1930.

Beginning with Nebraska Public Power District, habitat conservation efforts have spanned decades and organizations.

In the 1980s, the Crane Trust and The Nature Conservancy worked in conjunction with the US Fish and Wildlife Service in conservation efforts like clearing trees and controlling invasive species. In 2010, sloughs were dug in the cleared area.

Ownership and management activities have since been passed to the Platte River Recovery Implementation Program. They work today to spray invasive vegetation like phragmites in the channel and other riparian areas, remove brush and invasive woody vegetation from islands and grassland areas, clear trees back from the channel banks to improve open sight distance and encourage channel widening, and construct and manage in-channel nesting islands.

The Elm Creek Complex has change dramatically over the last 80 years.
