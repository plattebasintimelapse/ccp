The Tale of Water

By Don Welch, Nebraska poet

There is a moment when

the eyes of the water grow violet,

when like amethysts, blue diamonds,

they see from a summit,

and the air is full of alarm--

something has happened to water,

it has been caught in an ache,

frozen in angles, needled in crystals.

On the edge of the mountains,

in towering falls of water,

there are long frozen tongues.

In nuclear winter

how long would the leaves be green

in your memory?

Even the weed sings, wearing clear pearls,

orbed in original energy.

This is the tale of water.

Listen hard.
